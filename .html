About Me

My research explores the role of emotional salience on sensory processing, attention, and behaviour. Specifically, I target the mechanisms by which multisensory emotional information can impact actions and perception and strive to understand how raw sensory information can interact with emotional states and clinical disorders.

Originally from Northern Ontario, I spent my formative years around the Kirkland Lake area before moving to London, Ontario to begin postsecondary education. My research career began as an undergraduate research assistant in the Cerebral Systems Lab at the University of Western Ontario (now Mcgill, PI: Dr. Stephen Lomber). Here, I worked with animal models to investigated how the brain can change in response severe sensory impairment, and how these changes can be understood by monitoring perception and behaviour1.

Moving into graduate studies, my focus shifted outside of animal models, and into the area of human cognitive neuroscience. Working from the Brain and Mind Institute under the supervision of Dr. Derek Mitchell, I completed my PhD in Neursoscience, with research that used a combination of functional neuroimaging and behavioural experiementation to investigate impact emotional information has on spatial perceptual2,3,4.

I completed a postdoctoral fellowship in the Motivated Cognition Lab under the guidance of Dr. Rebecca Todd at the University of British Columbia. Here, I continued my invesigation into the role of emotion in sensory processing5, albeit making a concerted effort to expand focus beyond audition and vision. My research has included outlining the neural pathways of emotional signals coded at the level of sensation rather than through centralized appraisal6, expanding our ability to analyze and interpret human fMRI data7 and redefining our understanding of specifitiy in autonomic response8.

In my role as Assistant Professor at Lakehead University, I continue to query the interplay between sensation and emotion. My current methodologies include human fMRI, wearable psychophysiological recording, immersive multisensory environment (including virtual reality) and behavioural experimetation. My goal is to understand the neurocognitive mechanism that colour our world with emotional meaning, altering our perceptions and influencing our behaviour.
